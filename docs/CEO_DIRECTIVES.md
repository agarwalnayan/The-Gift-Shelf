# CEO Directives

This document represents the permanent company directives for The Gift Shelf (TGS). Every future software engineer, AI assistant, and product manager must strictly adhere to these principles.

## Company Vision
* **Brand Identity**: The Gift Shelf is a premium D2C personalized gifting brand.
* **Competitive Advantage**: We compete on trust, premium presentation, exceptional customer experience, deep personalization, and seamless technology.
* **Scale Strategy**: We are focused on curated quality. We are **not** trying to become the largest gifting marketplace.

## Business Model
* **Fulfillment**: Asset-light supplier network.
* **Core Platform**: Website-first. The web platform is the primary engine of the business.
* **Acquisition & Support**: Instagram serves as the primary channel for Discovery, while WhatsApp acts as the primary channel for Customer Support.
* **Operational Control**: Admin-driven operations. Everything must be manageable by our team without engineering intervention.
* **Catalog Architecture**: Dynamic catalog. There must be absolutely no hardcoded products or categories in the codebase.

## North Star Metrics
Every decision should positively impact one or more of these approved metrics:
1. Website Conversion Rate
2. Customer Trust
3. Average Order Value
4. Repeat Purchase Rate
5. Customer Satisfaction

## Engineering Philosophy
Before proposing or writing any code, every engineering decision must answer:
* Does this improve customer trust?
* Does this improve website conversion?
* Does this improve customer experience?
* Is this scalable?
* Is this maintainable?
* Does this avoid unnecessary complexity?

## Software Principles
Permanent rules for the codebase and development lifecycle:
* **No Regeneration**: Never regenerate completed modules.
* **Architectural Continuity**: Continue with the existing architecture.
* **Systemic Thinking**: Build systems, not hardcoded features.
* **Admin-Centric**: Everything must be configurable through the Admin dashboard.
* **Scalability**: Prefer scalable, stateless solutions over quick hacks.
* **Clean Code**: Preserve clean architecture and separation of concerns.
